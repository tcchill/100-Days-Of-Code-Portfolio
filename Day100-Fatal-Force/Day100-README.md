# Day 100 – Fatal Force Data Analysis 🔍

An exploratory data analysis (EDA) of fatal police shootings in the US from 2015–2017, based on The Washington Post's database.

## Dataset
- Fatal shootings by US police (`Deaths_by_Police_US.csv`)
- US Census data: poverty rate, high school graduation rate, median household income, racial demographics

## Key Questions Explored
- What is the poverty rate and high school graduation rate across US states?
- What is the racial breakdown of people killed by police?
- Were the victims armed? What kind of weapons were involved?
- What is the age distribution of victims?
- Which cities and states have the highest number of police killings?
- Is there a trend in police killings over time?

## Visualisations
- Bar charts, pie/donut charts, choropleth map
- KDE plots and histograms for age distribution
- Line chart for killings over time

## Tech Stack
- Python 3, Pandas, NumPy
- Plotly Express, Matplotlib, Seaborn

## Key Findings
- The majority of victims were armed with guns (~55%)
- Black and White victims account for the largest share of fatalities
- Los Angeles, Phoenix, and Houston rank among the most dangerous cities
- No clear upward or downward trend in killings between 2015–mid 2017

## Data Source
[The Washington Post – Fatal Force Database](https://www.washingtonpost.com/graphics/investigations/police-shootings-database/)
